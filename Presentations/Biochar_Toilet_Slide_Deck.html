Infrastructure Gap

Over 2.5 billion people lack access to basic sanitation. Traditional solutions fail because:

  • Water Scarcity: Flush toilets require massive amounts of potable water, making them impossible in arid regions.
  • Pathogen Risk: Composting toilets require expert management over long periods to be safe.
  • Energy Costs: Standard incineration is too expensive for rural clinics/homes.

Phase 1: Proof of Concept & Logic

Architecture & Dryness Detection

The core challenge is energy efficiency. Drying and charring are two separate energy stages. The system must detect exactly when moisture has been eliminated to minimize power waste.

  • Heat Source: Silicon Nitride igniter / modified pressure vessels.
  • Control: ESP32 with Solid State Relays (SSR).
  • Sensing: Calibrated thermocouples and pressure sensors mapping internal divergence.
The "Flash" Decompression: At 120°C+ under pressure, a specialized solenoid valve suddenly opens. Liquid water inside pathogens instantly turns to steam, causing them to explode. 100% Sterility Achieved mechanically.

Phase 2: Induction System Development

Rapid Joule Heating via Induction

Transitioning from resistive heating to Pulsed Induction Heating to overcome the thermal inefficiency of processing high liquid volumes.

  • Contactless Energy Transfer: Heat is generated directly within the internal charring cup.
  • Accelerated Processing: Reaches charring thresholds (300°C+) exponentially faster.
  • System Durability: Removes exposed heating coils from the corrosive waste environment.

Induction Innovations: Magnetite Matrix

The induction system goes beyond basic contactless heating. By utilizing a magnetite and clay-based medium as the magnetic susceptor, we achieve major breakthroughs in processing physics:

1. Massive Surface Area

The clay acts as a highly effective absorber, distributing liquid waste (feces and urine) evenly throughout the entire substrate. This creates an optimal surface area to wet mass ratio.

2. Rapid Dewatering

A relatively low induction frequency provides even volumetric heating (except at the containment walls). This triggers rapid boiling and steam generation, drastically accelerating the dewatering phase.

Inherent Safety:
The Curie Effect

Magnetite has a fixed Curie temperature. Once the substrate reaches a specific thermal threshold, it naturally loses its magnetic properties and stops absorbing induction energy.

This provides a physical failsafe against thermal runaway, independent of software.

The Circular Economy & Open-Source Impact

Beyond Disposal: Net-Negative Sanitation

The byproduct of this process is not waste—it is a climate-positive resource. High-temperature pyrolysis locks carbon into a stable matrix that prevents methane release.

  • Agriculture: Biochar-amended soil holds 50% more moisture.
  • Nutrient Sponge: Prevents fertilizer runoff during heavy rains.
  • Carbon Sink: Sequesters carbon in the soil for 100+ years.
